Today we had to submit an online presentation, Levon and Lenart handled this, creating and presenting it. It was a live pitch of our project, where project partners and academics viewed our 2-minute presentation. It went very well and some inquired further about our product via email.

(embed the video maybe)

Later in the week, we had a meeting with Sheena Visram that lasted 30 minutes where we showed her our product, asked for advice, and implemented it. This discussion was very insightful as she has tons of experience in working with developers and producing applications that go out to those with specific needs in the NHS.

Discussing our project, she gave us several tips:

  • A community feature would be great
  • Think about how frequently the app is going to be used
  • Seek advice from visually impaired and target
  • How can we optimize their journey, and update live

All in all, she reassured us we are doing great, and beyond schedule, so it seems the project is going well.

At this point in the project, everything is going well. Amir is working on the report and trying to help on the frontend. Eric is working actively on the frontend, waiting for the backend to be finished so he can link the front end with the backend.
