Blog Update

21/12/24

January is getting closer, so we decided to lock in. We are now laser-focused on the technical development of our project since after some time communicating with clients, we have a clear idea of our requirements and have added some additional features we may be able to implement along with the core features.

Our progress can be summarized in the paragraph:

We are developing a comprehensive database to enhance urban accessibility, focusing initially on road-crossing features like zebra crossings, traffic lights, ramps, and curb widths. The database will support various resolutions, including integration with the what3words API. Our API will enable users to send data, add custom layers, and retrieve specific layers for their applications. Additionally, we are creating a user-friendly web app for data population and retrieval, and a widget for clients to integrate data into their websites. Future plans include implementing authentication for user-submitted updates, temporary warning layers, and exploring population density data. We aim to expand the database with new layers such as benches, ramps, and accessible toilets, and ensure the design allows for easy integration of data from other countries. Our goal is to make cities more accessible and inclusive for all.
