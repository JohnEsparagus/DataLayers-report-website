Blog Update

19/03/2025

Recent Developments & User Testing

Progress has continued with some key updates. We have added form functionality along with the backend endpoint to handle submissions. Grid lines are now displaying, though there are still some bugs. The issue isn’t with W3W but rather with how the overlay grid is being rendered in the map component. Additionally, pop-ups now include graphs and can be moved dynamically.

Testing with children provided useful feedback, leading to improvements such as a collapsible sidebar for better navigation and larger font sizes for readability.

Final Fixes & Refinements

As the project moves into the final stretch, the team is focused on fixing the grid alignment issue, particularly in 3x3 views. We are also adding wheelchair-accessible locations, including graphs for accessibility features and warnings about hazards, ensuring the platform works on mobile devices, and refining the UI, including icons and overall visual improvements.

User Feedback & Future Considerations

Some suggested changes are relatively easy to implement, such as:

  • Adding a miles/time option.
  • Creating a dedicated warnings layer.
  • Making the zoom-to-focus mode larger and brighter.
  • Slowing down text-to-speech and using a British accent.

Other suggestions, like building a fully custom navigation tool, integrating real-time protest alerts, or making the widget more interactive are more complex and will require additional research.

Final Documentation & Transparency

To document the project, the team is preparing two videos:

  • "How We Built This" – An 8-minute look at the development process.
  • Final Monthly Video – A 2-3 minute extended elevator pitch featuring real users.

Additionally, the team will be clear about where GPT was used during development to maintain transparency.

With only a few weeks left, the focus is now on final refinements and ensuring the platform is ready for launch.
