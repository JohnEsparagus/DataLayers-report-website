Blog Update

7/11/2024

Redefining Our HCI Approach

Recently, we had to revisit and refine our Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) approach due to changing requirements. This led us to create additional sketches and prototypes to better align with our new objectives. We discovered that our focus should be on developing an embeddable widget for developers rather than a standalone application.

New Requirements and Database Enhancements

We have outlined a new set of requirements for our project:

Our database now includes an API that receives coordinates, object types, and reliability flags. This data is structured in JSON format and loaded into our database with multiple resolution options. We aim to have data layers for traffic lights, crossings, and wheelchair repair stations ready by January 21st.

The frontend widget for developers will feature clear documentation, a Google-Maps-like interface, and a dropdown menu for selecting data layers. Users can choose the resolution of the data points, whether coordinates or what3words tags.

On Hold for Confirmation

Currently, we are on hold as we confirm our requirements and communicate with clients and Dean. Although there have been minor setbacks, they are not significant issues. During this time, our team has communicated extensively and has a clear understanding of the suitable roles for each member.
