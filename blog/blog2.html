Blog Update

October 2

These weeks, we worked really hard developing the HCI report. We planned to engage with clients and advisors including Soundscape, Nick, GDI Hub Lab, and Balquis. Our discussions focused on developing a database/website where users can upload photos and objects to enhance our data layer. We considered mechanisms to ensure user trustworthiness and explored the integration of What3words, with Balquis pointing us to relevant datasets. We expect data contributions from Team 15 by January. Our plans include working on an outdoor layer with hot and cold warnings, and developing basic frontend features for authentication and authorization. Lastly, we clarified that DEAN is the client.

What we have accomplished

  • Gathering End User requirements
  • Conducting interviews with software engineers and the visually impaired.
  • Creating technical requirements such as geospatial data and front end accessibility features.

Insights and Personas

We developed personas modeling user requirements, bringing them to life with motivations, pain points, and ideal features. We were taught the importance of personas in detail, they bring life to the needs of users and include detailed information about the target audience, also it allows us to empathize with our personas. Developing the personas was deeply personal.

What we plan to do

  • Our user requirements have suddenly changed completely, as a result we would have to reform our HCI and contact the dean and wheelchair alliance for more clarity
  • Get started on the widget and backend technical infrastructure for our project.
  • Communicate with team 14 on requirements and how we could work together in the coming months
