Abstract

Accessibility is a key issue for many people with disabilities. This project aims to provide a solution to this problem by creating a system that can be used by people with disabilities to navigate their environment. The system will use data layers to provide information about the environment, such as the location of obstacles and accessible routes. This information will be presented to the user in a way that is easy to understand and navigate. The system will also provide feedback to the user to help them navigate their environment more effectively. The project will be evaluated by testing it with people with disabilities to ensure that it meets their needs and is easy to use.

Maybe I'll Make This on a Pretty Website Later

Updates every 2 weeks on project progress

  • 21/12/24
  • 12

These last 2 weeks, we worked really hard developing the HCI report. In weeks 1 and 2, we planned to engage with clients and advisors including Soundscape, Nick, GDI Hub Lab, and Balquis. Our discussions focused on developing a database/website where users can upload photos and objects to enhance our data layer. We considered mechanisms to ensure user trustworthiness and explored the integration of What3words, with Balquis pointing us to relevant datasets. We expect data contributions from Team 15 by January. Our plans include working on an outdoor layer with hot and cold warnings, and developing basic frontend features for authentication and authorization. Lastly, we clarified that DEAN is the client.
